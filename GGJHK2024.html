The Global Game Jam (GGJ) is an annual distributed game jam. At each site, participants gather to develop ideas, form small groups, create new, creative, innovative games, and present them to their peers and the global community, all in a limited time span.

In 2024, the game jam was hosted in hybrid format, over the course of a week. I worked in a team of 1 artist, 4 programmers and 1 game designer.

Project details

A multiplayer party game to determine the age old debate: Which game first - Chicken or the Egg?

  • Scan the QR code to play!
  • Host up to 20 friends under local connection
  • Pick up different powerups and traps
  • Randomly generated map every time you play
  • Too easy? Make the map more difficult with joystick configurations
  • Reach the finish line first to win!
So... Which came first? Chicken or the egg? You decide!


I was responsible for the project management, art direction, game assets, game trailer & GUI.

Game Trailer

We only had a 1 minute trailer to showcase our game as the Hong Kong Jam site consistently has over 50 projects.

To maximize the time used for development, I asked our programmers for footage of the game during their bug testing so I can edit it into the trailer in advance. As the visuals did not give enough context of how our game worked, I also wrote a script for a voice over. I added footage of players trying the game out to demonstrate what we envisioned the game to play like; and as a strong piece of evidence that it actually was playable!

Project Tracking

I used Trello to track these tasks and progress so everyone was on the same page.

As our team had members with varied capabilities, interests and availabilty, we first took the time to get to know each other. I then invited everyone to help list all the tasks we had to finish to complete the game. As we only had a limited amount of time, I led the conversation on differentiating the core tasks from stretch goals, and asked team members to take up on the tasks that they were comfortable working on. To ensure that we were on track, I also regularly checked on their progress to see if they ran into any problems and encouraged conversation among other team members to see if other members had the expertise to help troubleshoot.

Won: Best Dynamic Content Game Award!
